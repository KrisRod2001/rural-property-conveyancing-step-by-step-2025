Phase 1: Pre-Contract (Weeks –4 to 0)

What most people get wrong: Waiting until the contract is signed to involve a conveyancer.

What you must do instead:
✓ Appoint your rural conveyancer the moment you shortlist a property
✓ Order preliminary title + plan search (cost ~$120)
✓ Run Water Access Licence search (free online in most states)
✓ Check Native Title Tribunal map (takes 2 minutes)
✓ Identify if it’s freehold, Crown lease, or Western Lands lease
Typical time: 1–3 weeks before making an offer

Phase 2: Contract Exchange (Day 0)

Critical special conditions you MUST insert:
  • Subject to satisfactory water entitlement verification
  • Subject to Crown Lands consent (if leasehold)
  • Subject to Clean Energy Regulator project transfer (if carbon registered)
  • Subject to satisfactory contamination and heritage searches
  • Vendor to provide full 10-year water trading history
Cooling-off (NSW): 5 business days | QLD: 5–7 days | VIC: 3 days

Phase 3: Due Diligence Period (Weeks 1–12)

Week 1–2: Full searches package
Week 3–6: Water confirmation letter from WaterNSW / Sunwater / Goulburn-Murray Water
Week 4–8: Environmental & contamination reports (if required)
Week 6–10: Crown Lands consent application (if leasehold)
Week 8–12: Finance approval (rural loans take longer)
Average rural due diligence: 10–14 weeks (vs 4–6 weeks residential)

Phase 4: Pre-Settlement (Weeks 12–20)

  • Final water account adjustment
  • Rates & land tax adjustments
  • Stock & plant valuation (if included)
  • Transfer documents signed via PEXA (or wet ink for leases)
  • Final Crown Lands consent received
  • Carbon project transfer consent (if applicable)

Phase 5: Settlement Day

Typical settlement times for rural property:
Freehold only → same-day electronic settlement possible
Any Crown lease → usually 2–5 business days after all consents
Western Lands leases → often 30–90 days after consent

You receive: Keys, water accounts, bore keys, gate combinations, carbon project logins, pest/weed management plans.
